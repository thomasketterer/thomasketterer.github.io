About Me

    Hey, I’m Thomas Ketterer, a college student at UNC Chapel Hill with a passion for cybersecurity, video design, and philosophy. I’m majoring in mathematics and constantly exploring ways to bridge mathematical theory with real-world security applications. My interests lie at the intersection of logic, creativity, and problem-solving, which fuel my curiosity in everything from penetration testing and ethical hacking to digital design and emerging tech trends.
    I love using my creativity to build and experiment with new ideas—whether it’s designing interactive projects, working on hands-on security labs, or developing programs that push my understanding of technology. My background in mathematics gives me a structured approach to tackling complex problems, but I also enjoy thinking outside the box and teaching myself new skills. Whether it’s learning a new programming language, diving into design software, or exploring abstract philosophical concepts, I thrive on continuous learning.
    Beyond the technical side, I stay engaged through Model UN, where I sharpen my analytical and communication skills, as well as reading articles and textbooks that challenge my perspective. I’m always looking for new ways to apply what I learn, whether through personal projects, research, or collaboration.
    Check out my projects below or reach out if you’d like to connect — I’d love to chat about cybersecurity, design, or anything in between!
